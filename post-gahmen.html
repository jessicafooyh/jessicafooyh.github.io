Gah-men Speak: LSTM

Predicting the Singaporean government's responses

Posted by Jessica

Inspired by my friend's project on modelling Straits Times articles, I decided to do the same on various government-produced text in Singapore. Like him, I relied mostly on this word-rnn source code to train a word-based, multi-layer Long-Short Term Memory (LSTM) network on politicians' answers in parliamentary sessions and ministries' press releases. I scraped most of them not actually from Hansard (because I'd then have to spend a lot of time cleaning it), but from individual ministries'/stat boards' News Centres (and focused only on the economic ones). I had only about 4MB of text data in total, which probably explains why a lot of text doesn't make grammatical sense and veers off-topic/seed often. While the generated text is a far cry from the type of answers we demand from our political leaders, I find it really interesting how the model picked up a lot of "government speak" (e.g., 'cost pressures in China', 'good jobs for Singaporeans', 'increase competitiveness'), catch phrases frequently used in government publications, and even the (sometimes convoluted) sentence structures.
