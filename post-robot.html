Robot Amok: EKF-SLAM

Modeling uncertainty in robotic motion

Posted by Jessica

This was one of the class assignments I quite enjoyed working on at a grad level course at the Toyota Technological Institute of Chicago (TTIC), TTIC 31170 - Planning, Learning and Estimation for Robotics and Artificial Intelligence, by Professor Walter. To be frank, I thoroughly enjoyed this course - it exposed me to a class of algorithms I hadn't learned in my statistics courses (i.e., reinforcement learning) and amidst all the theoretical homework I'd to do for my Statistics courses, this was a breath of fresh air - I got to spend hours just coding, and seeing probabilistic equations come to live.

For this project, we had to use the Extended Kalman Filter and Simultaneous Localisation and Mapping (EKF-SLAM) to jointly estimate a robot's position, as well as objects around it, given noisy measurements from a sensor like a camera and known robotic movement (e.g., a robot can only move 5cm from its current position in a second). The green dot represents the robot's ground-truth pose, and the green X-es are the true locations of the objects around it. The red and black dots are our estimates of the robot's pose and object locations respectively, after accounting for uncertainty from the robot's movement and measurement (sensors). The colourful circles represent our range of uncertainty about the estimates (something like confidence intervals). As the robot explores more of the area around it, the variance estimates shrink (circles become smaller), because we become more confident of the locations of the objects around it.
